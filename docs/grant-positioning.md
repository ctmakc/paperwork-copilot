# Grant Positioning

Last updated: 2026-04-16

## Why this product is better for credits than generic AI consulting

Paperwork Copilot has a cleaner credits story because it is:

- narrow
- repetitive
- operational
- measurable

It is not "AI transformation" in the abstract.

It is a specific workflow product with repeated model usage.

## Best narrative

We are building an AI paperwork copilot for service businesses.

The first wedge is HVAC contractors in the US and Canada, where inbound requests, estimates, invoices, and follow-ups are often still rebuilt manually from messy calls, texts, emails, and technician notes.

The product does not make autonomous financial or dispatch decisions. It helps operators normalize intake, detect missing job details, draft estimate and invoice paperwork, and prepare customer communication faster with human review.

## Why this is a good credits use case

Credits would be used for:

- intake normalization across messy raw inputs
- structured extraction from calls, notes, and emails
- missing-info detection and clarifying-question generation
- estimate and invoice draft generation
- multilingual and quality benchmarking across providers

This is a strong fit for credits because usage is:

- real
- recurring
- document-heavy
- easy to benchmark on cost and quality

## Strongest product qualities for grant reviewers

- clear SMB pain
- low-risk human-in-the-loop workflow
- narrow vertical wedge
- easy demo story
- measurable business value

## What to avoid in grant copy

Do not lead with:

- "AI for field service"
- "full operating system"
- "autonomous quoting"
- "replace your existing stack"

Lead with:

- faster paperwork
- cleaner estimate generation
- invoice drafting from messy notes
- fewer missing details before the quote goes out

## One-line pitch

Paperwork Copilot helps service contractors turn messy job intake into estimate drafts, invoice drafts, and customer follow-ups in minutes.

## Short application answer

We are building Paperwork Copilot, an AI paperwork assistant for service businesses. Our first wedge is HVAC contractors in the US and Canada. The product turns messy calls, texts, emails, and technician notes into structured intake records, flags missing information, drafts estimate and invoice paperwork, and prepares client follow-ups with human review. Credits would let us benchmark providers, improve extraction and drafting quality, and ship a focused vertical workflow product rather than spend early capital on repeated model testing.

## Why now

Small contractors already use a mix of phones, inboxes, basic field-service tools, and accounting software, but paperwork still breaks across those gaps.

That makes this a good AI wedge:

- the inputs are messy
- the outputs are repetitive
- the human stays in charge
- the ROI is immediate

## Best-fit target programs

This product is strongest for:

- Google startup / AI credits
- OpenAI startup ecosystem or relationship-building
- Anthropic only if a real startup access path exists

It is also a decent fallback fit for:

- cloud credits where the narrative values vertical SMB productivity and repeated model usage

## What the product must show before applying

- clean landing page
- one crisp reviewer flow
- sample intake to estimate demo
- sample completion note to invoice demo
- clear statement that final sending and pricing remain human-controlled
